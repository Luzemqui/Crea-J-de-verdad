Accompaniment on the Journey

To support someone with cancer, the key is authentic presence and avoiding forced positivity that invalidates their fears Listen actively without judgment, allowing the person to safely express their sadness or anger. Instead of asking "How can I help?" offers specific actions such as bringing food, cleaning, or running specific errands.

Always respect her autonomy, treating her as the person she is and not just as a patient undergoing treatment. Avoid giving unsolicited medical advice or sharing stories of other cases that might create unnecessary pressure. Silent support is often more comforting than platitudes or motivational speeches.
