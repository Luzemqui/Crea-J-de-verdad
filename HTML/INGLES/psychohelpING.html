Psychological Support

A diagnosis of a high-impact disease such as cancer profoundly disrupts the emotional stability of patients and their families. The psychological impact can give rise to feelings of uncertainty, fear, frustration, and constant isolation.


Navigating this process requires specialized therapeutic support that validates emotions, offers practical resilience tools, and fosters a safe, supportive space where the patient does not feel alone in their process of adaptation.

Professional Guidance

Psycho-oncological therapy assists patients in managing the anxiety, fear, and depression that arise following diagnosis, promoting a better quality of life and strengthening their mental health during treatment.

Family Support

The immediate inner circle also faces an overwhelming emotional burden. Joint sessions guide the family in affective communication, empathy, mutual care, and the collective management of daily stress.

Support Groups

Sharing experiences with people going through similar situations alleviates feelings of loneliness, reduces isolation, and fosters a mutual exchange of optimism and useful coping strategies.

Relaxation Techniques

Learning practical exercises in meditation, guided breathing, and emotional regulation helps reduce physical and mental tension, gradually restoring a sense of control in the face of daily challenges.
