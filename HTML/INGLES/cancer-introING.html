Introduction to cancer

Cancer is not a single disease but a broad term used to describe a group of more than 200 different diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body. If this process is not controlled or detected early, it can compromise the functioning of vital organs and spread to other parts of the body.


Understanding the fundamentals of cellular development, risk factors, and the importance of early diagnosis is a crucial first step toward prevention, comprehensive patient support, and the reduction of associated risks.

How it originates?

It begins when the mechanisms controlling cellular DNA undergo mutations. Instead of aging and dying, these damaged cells survive and continue to divide unchecked, potentially forming masses known as tumors.

prevention

Many types of cancer can be prevented or mitigated by adopting healthy habits: maintaining a balanced diet, avoiding tobacco and alcohol consumption, protecting the skin from the sun, and engaging in daily physical activity.

Early Detection

Detecting abnormalities at an early stage through self-examinations, mammograms, or routine check-ups significantly increases the effectiveness of current medical treatments.

Common Treatments

Depending on the type and stage of the diagnosis, treatments vary and include approaches such as surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy, immunotherapy, and targeted therapies, aimed at halting or eradicating the affected cells.
